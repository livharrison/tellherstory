SCIENCE

The achievements of women in science are often overlooked by the more famous inventors. Behind the everyday items and ideas that we take for granted, there are a number of women who were pioneers in their field, breaking gender roles and expanding the realms of science.

Women have gained momentum in the field of science and STEM fields, but these fields are generally male dominated. However, these female role models have helped women today enter the growing fields of science and STEM.

ADA LOVELACE

1815 - 1852

London, England

Ada Lovelace was an early mathmematician who is considered to be the first computer programmer. She was educated in mathematics and science from a young age, and she began to study with famous English Mathematician, Charle Babbage. She worked with him on his "Analytical engine," a proposed mechanical computer.
