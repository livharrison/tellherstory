POLITICS

The very first arguments for women's rights were made in the political sphere when women realized that they could not change anything without political power. The earliest women's suffrage movements were in the 1840's, with the Seneca Falls Convention. Women gathered and drafted a "Declaration of Sentiments", listing grievances and requests of the female sex. The signers of this document did not see the results during their lifetime, but the convention and spirits of these women helped ratify the 19th amendment, giving women the vote over 5 decades later.

With the first female vice president, there have been major advancements in women's role in politics. Without the work of these early women, we would not have equal voting rights and opportunities in the political sphere.
