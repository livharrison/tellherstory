LITERATURE

Women have contributed to many literary genres and periods throughout the years. Some of the most famous are Mary Shelley, Agatha Christie, and Virginia Woolf. Their books have entered into the world of historical writing and have gained popularity through the years.

Early female writers often wrote about feminist ideas, using strong independent female characters or the contrast of oppressed women to share their views. Especially with the writings concerning women's suffrage, many consider women's writing to be it's own genre.

JUDITH SARGENT MURRAY

1751 - 1820

Massachusetts, USA

Judith Sargent Murray was one of the early American's advocating for women's rights. She wrote plays, essays, and letters to spread her ideas. Her literary abilities helped propel her belief that women and men had equal intelligence and could thus acheive the same level of independence.

MERCY OTIS WARREN

1728 - 1814

Massachusetts, USA

Mercy Otis Warren was an early writer and political activist during the American Revolution. Her education was more than most women at the time, and she regularly wrote letters to influential people such as John and Abigail Adams. She wrote plays, letters, and books on revolutionary and gender issues. She is one of the first women that wrote to the general public.

HARRIET BEECHER STOWE

1811 - 1896

Connecticut, USA

Harriet was an author and abolitionist. She was most well-known for writing her book "Uncle Tom's Cabin", which exposed the harsh conditions that slaves faced in her day.

MARY WOLLSTONECRAFT

1759 - 1797

London, England

Mary was a writer and advocate for social issues including womens' rights. Her 1792 book 'A Vindication of the Rights of Woman' is considered a classic feminist text.
